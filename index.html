About This Program

Day 1 — Heavy Bench (Weekend): A top single at RPE 7.5–8, then 4×5 back-offs at ~83%. This is your primary strength driver toward 300. The single weight climbs +2.5 lbs every week.

Day 2 — OHP (Monday): Overhead Press 4×5, climbing toward 4×8 before adding weight. OHP builds shoulder and tricep strength that carries directly to your bench.

Day 3 — Volume Bench (Wednesday): 4×6 at ~74% of training max. Builds work capacity and muscle. Weight increases +2.5 lbs each week alongside the heavy day.

Day 4 — Speed Bench (Thursday): 8×3 at 65% with 60–90s rest. Every rep is explosive. Speed work builds bar velocity and motor pattern — critical for the 300 lb single.

Hypertrophy: 4 sets of 1 exercise per muscle group (back, shoulders, biceps, triceps) each day = 16 sets/week per muscle. Weight progresses automatically when you hit the top of the rep range.

Deload (every 4th week): Weights drop to 60–70%. Recovery is when you actually grow stronger.

Failure Protocol: Miss the heavy single twice in a row → training max drops 10% and rebuilds. Assistance fails → hold weight, lower rep target before progressing again.
