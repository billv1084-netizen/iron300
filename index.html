About This Program

Day 1 — Heavy Bench: Top single at your Training Max (TM). RPE after the single drives next week's TM: RPE ≤7 → +5 lbs, RPE 8 → +2.5 lbs, RPE 8.5–9 → hold, RPE 10 → −2.5 lbs. Four back-off sets at 85% TM follow.

Day 2 — Volume Bench: 4×6 at ~74% TM. Rate the last set RPE to auto-regulate next week's weight. Builds the work capacity and muscle to support the heavy single.

Day 3 — OHP: Overhead Press, 4×5 building toward 4×8 before adding weight. Keeps shoulders and triceps healthy and strong.

Day 4 — Paused Bench: 5×4 at 75% TM with a mandatory 2-second pause at the chest. Builds leg drive, starting strength, and positional control — the things that matter most at 90%+ of max.

Assistance: One rowing movement, one rear delt, one curl, and one tricep isolation per day — except Day 4 (Paused Bench), which skips direct tricep work to protect the elbow after the most joint-intensive bench variation. Weight progresses automatically when you hit the top of the rep range across all sets.

Deload (every 5th week): Weights drop to 60–70%. This is when adaptation happens.

Failure Protocol: Miss the heavy single twice in a row → TM drops 10% and rebuilds. Assistance stalls → hold weight, adjust rep target.
